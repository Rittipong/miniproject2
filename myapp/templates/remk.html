Black Myth: Wukong is a visually stunning and mechanically sound RPG that offers a fresh spin on a beloved myth. Its engaging combat, especially the epic boss battles, and impressive art direction make it a must-play for fans of action RPGs. However, the game's linear exploration, underwhelming gear system, and occasional technical issues hold it back from being a true masterpiece. For those looking for a challenging action RPG with deep roots in Chinese mythology, Black Myth: Wukong delivers an unforgettable experience, even if it stumbles in a few areas.

IGN Japan

Black Myth: Wukong's combat system doesn't have anything that makes it truly unique. The variety in ways to tackle combat is often lacking, and it doesn't feel all that good to play either. That said, the concept of creating openings for the enemy rather than exploiting them is interesting, and by rewarding players who take full advantage of the few chances the game gives you, it makes its boss encounters worthwhile despite its lacking combat system. Character customization and exploration is on the thin side too, and the story is hard to follow if you haven't read Journey to the West. However, the impressive graphics make every moment of the game look like a painting. Experiencing this world based on classic Chinese literature through the game's unique vision is still worth your time.

Wukong is my pick for GOTY - not for the story or it's combat but more for the overall experience and beautiful graphics. This is probably the prettiest game I've ever played. I will not be delving into the story. Not that there isn't one however it is hard to follow. The combat is limited to a certain degree in that you only ever use one combo however, there are countless ways to create builds based on the souls you collect, which each grant a unique ability and passive effect, spells, staff stances, armor sets, etc. Every streamer I've seen play the game used different sets of abilities and spells. So, although the combat is limited in some aspects, there are other aspects that make up for it by allowing for more customizable and varied builds seen from player to player. Wukong also encourages experimentation with your builds or changing on-the-fly to coincide with different boss encounters as you can respec at any time. If you can see past the few non-game-breaking flaws, Wukong is an experience that should not be missed.
